Tips for PhD Student

Publish Date: May 7, 2014    Last Update: May 7, 2014

Below are some tips I got from a professor on PhD study. These may not be applicable for everyone, but they are indeed worth reading.

  1. Don’t read the literature. Don’t be frustrated when you later find your idea has already been explored. It means your thinking is on the right track.
  2. Don’t write with faculty member. You spend a huge number of time, yet getting rewards that are not proportional.
  3. Start writing immediately. Your first paper is always crappy. What you can do it to have your first crappy paper at an early stage.
  4. Observe the world, and think of ideas. Insightful media could be a good source.
  5. The key to succeed in the job market is to have a VERY good job market paper,and a good presentation. Also don’t coauthor your job market paper.
  6. Keep a journal of ideas.
  7. Periodically go to seminars of another profession, to get fresh perspectives
  8. Meet with faculty members periodically, and use it as discipline tool to push yourself to do something. You can get good critics from Professors.
  9. Never be constrained by the data. Idea drives the paper. Start constructing an ideal dataset – and then look for the second best, the third best…until you find some data that could help you to implement your idea.
  10. Collect new data.
